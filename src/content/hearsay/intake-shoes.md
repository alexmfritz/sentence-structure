They take your shoes at intake and give you a pair of orange slip-ons sized by vibe. Mine were two sizes too big. The man next to me got two sizes too small. We were not permitted to trade, because trading was contraband, because in here everything that resembles a free exchange between two consenting adults is, technically, contraband.

So we both just walked wrong for a week. He shuffled. I clomped. The guard who processed us called it "the intake gait" and said you can spot a new arrival from across the yard by the way the shoes don't fit. He said it like weather. Like it was simply a fact of the season.

I have thought about those shoes more than I have thought about some people I love. That is not a confession I am proud of. It is just true, and Hearsay is the column where the true and the small get to live.
